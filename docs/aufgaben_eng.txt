Exercises in experiment 608 (Supernova)
===================================

1. Discussion of the spectrum on days 2, 10 and 50
   - How does the spectrum change (brightness, flux distribution, etc)?
   - Where do you see continuum radiation?
   - Where do you see absorption lines?
   - Where do you see emission lines?
   - What elements/ionization levels do you expect to see in the spectrum?
   - How large are the radial velocities typically?

2. Determine radial velocities and spectroscopic radii
   For days 2, 5, 10, 20, 30, ..., 100, determine the radial velocities of the absorption minimum of:
   - H-alpha (6563 AA)
   - Na I-D (5890 AA)
   - Ba II-2 (6141 AA)
   - Fe II-42 (5018 AA)
   
   Convert these into spectroscopic radii in units of solar radii.
   Discuss the stratification found.

3. Determine effective temperature
   - Fit Planck curves to the observed spectra for the tabulated days.
   - Calculate the photosphere radius from temperature using the Stefan-Boltzmann law.
   - Determine the Wien temperature from the continuum maximum.
   - Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of both methods.

4. Determine the distance to SN 1987A
   Compare the spectroscopic radii (from task 2) with the photosphere radii (from task 3).
   Use this comparison to determine the true distance D to the supernova.

5. Energy budget of SN 1987A
   Estimate how much energy SN 1987A emitted as electromagnetic radiation in the first 100 days.
   Compare this with:
   - The kinetic energy of the ejected shell (v ~ 10^4 km/s, M ~ 10 M_sun = 2 × 10^31 kg)
   - The energy of the neutrino pulse (Hint: the IMB detector in the USA detected 8 neutrinos with 30 MeV each)
   Discuss the result.

Don't forget about error propagation when calculating uncertainties.
When uncertainties are not given you can assume a reasonable error
(e.g., finding the continuum maximum with 10 angstrom precision).
